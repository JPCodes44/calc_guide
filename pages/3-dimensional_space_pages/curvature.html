Chapter 1.9 : Curvature

The curvature of a smooth curve at a point measures the rate at which the tangent vector to the curve is rotating at that point. It is defined as the magnitude of the rate of change of the unit tangent vector with respect to the arc length. The curvature indicates how much the curve deviates from a straight line at a particular point, with a curve having zero curvature being a straight line and a curve with high curvature bending sharply at that point.

The concept of curvature is important in differential geometry. It has many practical applications in fields such as physics and engineering, helping to understand the behavior of objects moving along curved paths.

The general equation for determining the curvature of a function is:

\(\kappa = \left\| {\dfrac{{d\,\vec T}}{{ds}}} \right\|\)

Given that \(T\) is the unit tangent and \(s\) is the arc length, we can also re-parameterize a curve to get it into terms of the arc length.

These formulas are:

\(\kappa = \dfrac{{\left\| {\vec T'\left( t \right)} \right\|}}{{\left\| {\vec r'\left( t \right)} \right\|}}\hspace{1.0in}\kappa = \dfrac{{\left\| {\vec r'\left( t \right) \times \vec r''\left( t \right)} \right\|}}{{{{\left\| {\vec r'\left( t \right)} \right\|}^3}}}\)

Although these equations are complex, it is still a benefit that we do not have to change the parameterization of the unit tangent.

Example 2

Determine the curvature for \(\vec{r} \left(t \right) = \left\langle t,2 sin \left(t \right), 2cos \left(t \right) \right\rangle\).

Show Solution

Let's first find the tangent and unit tangent vector:

\(\begin{align*}\vec r'\left( t \right) & = \left\langle {1,3\cos t, - 3\sin t} \right\rangle \\ \\ \vec T\left( t \right) & = \left\langle {\frac{1}{{\sqrt {10} }},\frac{3}{{\sqrt {10} }}\cos t, - \frac{3}{{\sqrt {10} }}\sin t} \right\rangle \end{align*}\)

\(\begin{align*}\vec r'\left( t \right) & = \left\langle {1,3\cos t, - 3\sin t} \right\rangle \\ \\ \vec T\left( t \right) & = \left\langle {\frac{1}{{\sqrt {10} }},\frac{3}{{\sqrt {10} }}\cos t, - \frac{3}{{\sqrt {10} }}\sin t} \right\rangle \end{align*}\)

\(\begin{align*}\vec r'\left( t \right) & = \left\langle {1,2\cos t, - 2\sin t} \right\rangle \\ \\ \vec T\left( t \right) & = \left\langle {\frac{1}{{\sqrt {5} }},\frac{2}{{\sqrt {5} }}\cos t, - \frac{2}{{\sqrt {5} }}\sin t} \right\rangle \end{align*}\)

Deriving the unit tangent, we get:

\(\vec T'\left( t \right) = \left\langle {0, - \frac{2}{{\sqrt {10} }}\sin t, - \frac{2}{{\sqrt {10} }}\cos t} \right\rangle\)

The norms are the following:

\(\begin{align*}\left\| {\vec r'\left( t \right)} \right\| & = \sqrt {1 + 4{{\cos }^2}t + 4{{\sin }^2}t} = \sqrt {5} \\ \\ \left\| {\vec T'\left( t \right)} \right\| & = \sqrt {0 + \frac{4}{{5}}{{\sin }^2}t + \frac{4}{{5}}{{\cos }^2}t} = \sqrt {\frac{4}{{5}}} = \frac{2}{{\sqrt {5} }}\end{align*}\)

The curvature is then,

\(\kappa = \dfrac{{\left\| {\vec T'\left( t \right)} \right\|}}{{\left\| {\vec r'\left( t \right)} \right\|}} = \dfrac{{\dfrac{2}{\sqrt {5} }}}{{\sqrt {10} }} = \dfrac{2}{{5}}\)

Example 1

Determine the curvature for \(\vec r \left( t \right) = t,\vec i + {t}^2\,\vec k\).

Show Solution

\(\vec r'\left( t \right) = \, \vec i + 2t\,\vec k\hspace{0.25in}\hspace{0.25in}\vec r''\left( t \right) = 2\,\vec k\)

Next, we will find the curvature:

\(\begin{align*}\vec r'\left( t \right) \times \vec r''\left( t \right) & = \left| {\begin{array}{*{20}{c}}{\vec i}&{\vec j}&{\vec k}\\ \\ {1}&0&2t\\0&0&2\end{array}} \right|\,\,\,\,\,\begin{array}{*{20}{c}}{\vec i}&{\vec j}\\{1}&0\\0&0\end{array}\\ \\ & = 2\vec j\end{align*}\)

The magnitudes are,

\(\left\| {\vec r'\left( t \right) \times \vec r''\left( t \right)} \right\| = 2\hspace{0.25in}\hspace{0.25in}\left\| {\vec r'\left( t \right)} \right\| = \sqrt {4{t^2} + 1}\)

The curvature at any value of \(t\) is then,

\(\kappa = \dfrac{2}{{{{\left( {4{t^2} + 1} \right)}^{\frac{3}{2}}}}}\)

Suppose we have a curve \(y=f(x)\) and want to find its curvature.

We can write its vector function as such:

\(\vec r\left( x \right) = x\,\vec i + f\left( x \right)\vec j\)

Then using the 2nd formula for curvature, we get this formula:

\(\kappa = \dfrac{{\left| {f''\left( x \right)} \right|}}{{{{\left( {1 + {{\left[ {f'\left( x \right)} \right]}^2}} \right)}^{\frac{3}{2}}}}}\)
