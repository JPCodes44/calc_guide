Facts

The physical interpretation for divergence represents the rate of change at which a vector field flows into or out of a point in \(\left(x,y \right)\) \(\mathbb{R}^2\) or \(\left(x,y,z \right)\) \(\mathbb{R}^3\). We can think of fluid vectors. If we have an incompressible fluid, the divergence is \(0\) because the liquid does not flow in or out of a specific source (the fluid is not being compressed).

The physical interpretation for a curl of a vector field can be described as the swirling motion of water in a pool. For example, If a vector field shows the water moving in a circular motion and we place a toy boat in the pool, it will tend to rotate. The amount of rotation will depend on the position and orientation of the object. Thus, the curl of the vector field is expected to be a vector with different values.
